The Bean Bar

There are four main coffee bean types enjoyed around the world! Tap on each name to learn more.

4 main beans enjoyed worldwide

ARABICA

Characteristics

  1. Oval shape and curved centre cut
  2. 2 varieties (Typica & Bourbon)
  3. Typica: More common & low-yielding
  4. Bourbon: Complex balanced aromas

History

  1. World oldest and most dominant coffee species
  2. About 55% world coffee

Taste

  1. smoother , sweeter taste
  2. Boast notes of chocolate and hints of fruits
  3. 1.1% caffeine by weight
  4. good for pour over and black coffee
  5. Sweet and smooth
  6. Chocolate and fruity hints
  7. Low caffeine (1.1%)
  8. Best for black coffee

Cost

  1. Very sensitive to environment
  2. Produce better fruits at higher altitude
  3. Hard to grow and is hand harvested
  4. Premium coffee beans

ROBUSTA

Characteristics

  1. Knowns as Canephora
  2. Small, Round, Straight middle cut
  3. Ideal for Espresso-based drinks and Commercial coffee blends

History

  1. Around 45% world coffee
  2. Vietnam is the largest producer

Taste

  1. Produced better crema, the creamy layer found on espresso shot
  2. 2-4.5% Caffeine by weight
  3. Strong, Harsher, Bitter, Taste like rubber, wood

Cost

  1. Grow at lower altitudes
  2. Half price cheaper than Arabica

Liberica

Characteristics

  1. larger than Arabica and Robusta beans, with a unique shape that resembles a teardrop
  2. Highest risk of fermentation

History

  1. Most are hybridized plants, hence pure liberica coffee is in danger of going extinct
  2. Abour 2% of global coffee consumption

Taste

  1. 1.23% of caffeine by weight
  2. Sweetest and most aromatic coffee
  3. Range from fruity and floral notes to lactic aromas
  4. Highest sugar content of all coffee
  5. for adventurous drinkers

Cost

  1. Finicky to cultivate and difficult to harvest
  2. Grow mostly in southeast Asia
  3. Liberica coffee is the most exp

Excelsa

Characteristics

  1. its beans is around the same as robusta beans

History

  1. only around 1% global coffee consumption

Taste

  1. it have a Powerful, earthy profile
  2. 0.86%- 1.13% of caffeine by weight
  3. it is extremely niche
  4. for adventurous drinkers

Cost

  1. the most expensive coffee beans
  2. rarest coffee varieties globally

Brewing Basics

From worldwide classics to local favourites, these 7 brewing methods are the most common

7 brewing methods from classics to local favorites

Classic Brewing

Espresso
[Advance Brewing Method]

  • Require: An espresso machine
  • Caffiene: High, ~8.6 mg/g
  • Flavour: Bold, intense
  • Base for drinks: cappuccino, latte, americano

Kopi Sock
[Beginner Brewing Method]

  • Require: A coffee sock
  • Caffeine: Medium, ~100mg per cup
  • Flavour: Smooth, syrupy, velvety
  • Note: Used in all our traditional coffee

Instant Coffee
[Beginner Brewing Method]

  • Require: A coffee sock
  • Caffeine: Low, ~60mg per serving
  • Flavour: Weaker than fresh brew
  • Types: Soluble, Powdered, Granulated

Manual Brewing

Drip Coffee

  • Require: Drip coffee maker
  • Caffeine: Mediunm, ~90mg per serving
  • Flavour: Clean, Mellow
  • Notes: Automated process (not same as pour over)

Pour Over Coffee

  • Require: Manual pour-over setup
  • Caffeine: Medium, ~145mg per serving
  • Flavour: Rich, Delicate, Complex
  • Note: Pure taste, Not Automated

Immersion Brewing

French press

  • Require: Beaker + metal mesh plunger
  • Caffeine: Medium, ~12 mg/g
  • Flavour: Full-bodied, mild acidity
  • Types: Retains natural oils from beans

Cold Brew

  • Require: Long steeping (8 to 24h), cold water
  • Caffeine: Medium, ~100 mg per 8 oz
  • Flavour: Smooth, Low-Acidy
  • Types: Usually diluted before drinking
